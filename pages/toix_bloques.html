'Inventing Smart Toys for Kids: 'Toix Bloques', Motorized Building Blocks Labview. Fabrication


in collaboration with the Eliot-Pearson Children's School at Tufts University





As a final course project, my Intro to Engineering class was assigned with the task of creating a smart, technology-driven toy for kids. My group made a trip to the Eliot-Pearson Children's School to spend some time with the kids and talk to them about what they would like to see in their toys. ​ While many of them had various and crazy ideas, there was one thing which I found all these children to have in common: they all wanted a toy that could be somewhat customary. They also wanted their toy to move. ​ With inspiration from our trip to the local preschool, my group and I created "Toix Bloques", a motorized building block set which textured surfaces for easy attachment and color coding for facilitated comprehension. The toy set comes with individual blocks built from lazer-cut birchwood. "Special blocks" include rotating motors and LEDS which can be built onto with other pieces. Motors are controlled by a simple smartphone app using LabView.
