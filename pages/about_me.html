About Me






Hi- my name is Liliko Uchida (legally spelt 'Ririko' due to some discrepencies between English and Japanese phonetics), and I am a third year student at Tufts University in Medford, Massachusetts pursuing a dual degree in mechanical engineering and physics.

As an avid musician outside of my education, choosing an academic focus in college which would satisfy my passion for music while allowing me to make a societal difference in creative ways was very important to me. Engineering seemed to integrate these two things perfectly -- music would not be possible without the creative processes which go into audio-engineering tracks, fabricating new instruments, and designing innovative audio equipment for improved sound and fit. Aside from innovations in leisure topics, engineering serves as the core to modern problem solving and technologies. I am excited to see how studying mechanical engineering (and physics merely out of a sparked passion)  will enable me to contribute to my community in the future by using my skills and experience to create technologies leading to a higher quality of life and expanding bounds of creativity. 

Outside of classes, I enjoy spending my free time finding an artistic outlet (ie: writing, drawing, singing, etc...) or participating in outdoor recreation. Some of my favorite activities to do when the weather permits is paddle boarding on the local lake or playing sports with friends. 

I am a very self-driven individual who finds passion and motivation in everyday life, and I hope to find opportunities to apply these qualities. Gaining real-life experience in order to broaden my perspective while contributing my strengths to a shared goal is very important to me, and I look forward to the beginning of my journey collaborating with others to innovate new technologies and take on creative problem solving approaches.
