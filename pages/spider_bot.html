In attempt to make a robot which could climb from floor 0 of the Tufts Science and Engineering Complex to floor 1, my team ambitioned to take the unconventional route and build a wall-climbing spider-bot. To do so, we began by creating a simple driving robot controlled over wifi with an Arduino. Next, the goal was to decide in what method the robot would stick to the wall. We programmed the bot and selected bot wheels such that the robot can drive towards a vertical wall with enough momentum and have enough torque from the wheel-wall friction to lift itself vertically. From there, the idea was to attach drone motors inside the body to create a suction between the bot and the wall, holding it vertically while the wheels' friction navigated the robot upwards. Unfortunately, my group and I were not able to complete our full goal of the wall climbing robot in time for the project submission deadline. However, this project was a great opportunity for creativity and experiment!
